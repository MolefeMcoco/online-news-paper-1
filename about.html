About us

Mandate

Language.

Publish news and opinions in indigenous languages.

Knowledge.

Inform all societies in the communities we serve; providing reliable media that people can own.

Power.

Redress marginalization of disadvantaged communities and persons, from access to media and the industry.

Mission Statement

online Community Newspaper is independently for the people, who empower by inciting their neighbours with informative and positive local news and national headlines.

Values & Principles

We produce media that incorporates a “community journalism approach”; where content is informed by the needs of the community. In other words, media that gives a voice to communities as opposed to that of authorities. We publish and present media that promotes dialogue and social change about issues.

  • Independence: Media that is independent from corporate, government or donor control.
  • Social Justice: Media that challenges racism, sexism and xenophobia.
  • Access and Participation: Media that provide community access to means of production, including equipment and training opportunities.

PRESS CODE

online Community Newspaper strives to hold these rights in trust for the country’s citizens; and it is subject to the same rights and duties as the individual. Everyone has the duty to defend and further these rights, in recognition of the struggles that created them: the media, the public and government who all make up a democratic state.

Our work is guided at all times by the public interest, understood to describe information of legitimate interest or importance to citizens. As a newspaper, we commit ourselves to the highest standards of excellence to maintain credibility and keep the trust of our readers. This means always striving for truth, avoiding unnecessary harm, reflecting a multiplicity of voices in our coverage of events, showing a special concern for children and other vulnerable groups, and acting independently.

In essence: It is a requirement of the Code that all advertising should be legal, decent, honest and truthful, and be prepared with a sense of responsibility to the consumer. This newspaper has committed itself to report news truthfully and accurately, and to publish comment that is fair, in accordance with the highest standards of journalism as set out in the Press Code of South Africa.

Editorial Policies & Strategies

Policy

The Editor reserves the right to determine whether material submitted for publishing shall be printed and reserves the right to edit as needed, for space, libellous statements and personal unwarranted attacks. All material submitted must be signed. Names can be withheld upon request. Opinions expressed by the writers of the articles in the newspaper are those of the individual and not necessarily those of the Editor of the publication workforce.

Article submitted for publishing must deal with issues, not personalities. The Editor shall be responsible for all the material submitted to online Community Newspaper pertaining to spelling, grammar, violations of federal/State laws and regulations, length etc. At no time shall the Editor alter the motive, intent or direction of an article. Should any conflict arise, all reasonable efforts should be made to contact the writer(s). Articles may also be presented to the editorial management/publisher if deemed necessary.

Opinions expressed in online Community Newspaper are those of the writer(s) and do not necessarily reflect the views of the Editor, the executive board of Lolliepop Trading and Projects (Pty) Ltd (Publisher) online Community Newspaper reserves the right to make any changes necessary to accepted articles. Any and all opinions appearing in the newspaper are opinions of the writer(s) and do not necessarily reflect the views of the Editor, its advertising sponsors, partners and personnel associated with the publication.

Strategy

online Community Newspaper has been established as a strategic approach to transforming local media ownership and serves the majority of township communities. Our slogan: Language, Knowledge, Power has empowered ordinary South African from all walks of life; establishing a permanent audience that reliably trusts the media product it receives.

Language.

We provide accurate and fair news, current events, opinions and interest features – in languages and contexts the people understand. Our editorial content is predominantly sources through journalists, freelancers and area-based reporters. We use indigenous languages in most editorial content.

Knowledge.

We publish information that helps people become aware of services and programmes that have the potential to help in some way with problems they encounter in their lives. NOTE: It is often the case that a newspaper report highlighting a community member’s problem leads to the person getting empowered to receive help. Our team has developed a passion for sharing knowledge. Our duty is to educate, inform and entertain our readers consistently.

Power.

We are a media platform influence by social change. We utilize modern technological advancements to create a quality print product, engage with readers constantly.

  • Social Media: Facebook is the newspaper’s main open platform for communicating and sharing topics, news, alerts, notices, ads – encouraging active participation in the Internet to create world-wide social impact.

  • Web Site: Today, as never before, people are in a hurry. The entire technological revolution involving the Internet has a single focus: it is to increase the speed and reliability at which vital information is transmitted between and among interested parties. This site offers a Faster, Cheaper and Easier way of providing and sharing the vital local news, notices and happenings that impact our communities. Readers can quickly find information, read and write stories, share opinions and comment on a variety of subjects – 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, all year round.
